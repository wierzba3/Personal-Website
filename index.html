About Me

I began teaching myself programming in freshman year of college, at which time my major was in accounting. My first projects involved reverse engineering PC video games that I played which were repetative. I solved this repetitiveness problem by creating software that interfaced over the video games software (which I decompiled, deobfuscated) and injected my own code into, which allowed me to manipulate the fields in-memory and observe the state of the game via variable values in memory. This allowed me to send mouse and key events into the game to effectively play the game for me.


Shortly after I switched my major to computer science, as I knew I would enjoy and excel in this field much more. I graduated from UWM Milwaukee in 2014 and have worked in the field since 2013 starting with an internship with Wisconsin's utility company as a web application developer.


I enjoy working on personal projects when I have the time, which are listed below. Otherwise, I spend most of my time on my hobbies which include training for powerlifting, which I currently hold 9 state records in. I also enjoy travelling, hiking, camping, shooting guns/bows, and exploring the rocky mountains in my jeep.
